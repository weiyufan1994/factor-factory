## Function Definitions (Appendix A.2)
- sign(x) = standard definition (+1 for positive, -1 for negative, 0 for zero)
- delta(x, d) = today's value of x minus the value of x d days ago
- rank(x) = cross-sectional rank
- delay(x, d) = value of x d days ago
- correlation(x, y, d) = time-serial correlation of x and y for the past d days
- ts_rank(x, d) = time-series rank in the past d days

## Alpha#12 Context from the Paper
The paper categorizes alphas broadly into mean-reversion (contrarian) and momentum types.
Alpha#12 is listed in Appendix A as a standalone formula without explicit author commentary.
The paper notes these alphas are "price-volume" based (daily close-to-close returns, open, close, high, low, volume, vwap).
Average holding period: 0.6-6.4 days (short-horizon).
Average pair-wise correlation: 15.9% (low correlation across alphas).
Returns strongly correlated with volatility; no significant dependence on turnover.

## Author's General Alpha Description (Section 2)
"Very coarsely, one can think of alpha signals as based on mean-reversion or momentum.
A mean-reversion alpha has a sign opposite to the return on which it is based...
In complex alphas elements of mean-reversion and momentum can be mixed."

## What is known about Alpha#12:
- Formula: sign(delta(volume, 1)) * (-1 * delta(close, 1))
- Structure: sign(volume change direction) × (negative of price change direction)
- This is a contrarian/meAN-REVERSION type based on the paper's classification
- Delay: appears to be delay-1 (uses today's volume delta and today's price delta; to be traded next day)
- Holding period: likely short (1-2 days) based on the paper's general characterization of these alphas

## Intended interpretation of Alpha#12 signal logic:
- When volume increases (delta(volume,1) > 0, sign = +1), the signal amplifies (-1 * delta_close)
  - If price is up (delta_close > 0): signal is negative → short bias
  - If price is down (delta_close < 0): signal is positive → long bias
- When volume decreases (delta(volume,1) < 0, sign = -1), the signal flips
  - If price is up: signal is positive → long bias
  - If price is down: signal is negative → short bias
- Overall: signal is positive (long) when price moves AGAINST volume direction (price up on falling volume, or price down on rising volume)
- Signal is negative (short) when price moves WITH volume direction (price up on rising volume, or price down on falling volume)
- This is a volume-confirmation filter applied to price reversal

## Notes on the formula:
- sign(delta(volume,1)) is binary (+1/-1), discarding volume change magnitude
- The formula does NOT use: rank(), delay(), ts_rank(), or any time-series aggregation
- It is a pure 1-day signal based on first differences of volume and price
- Final transform (from paper convention): cross-sectional rank of the raw signal values
